# Intensity Score and Class Phase Assignments
## Research-Based Proposal for 35 Classical Pilates Movements

**Research Sources:**
- Classical Pilates 34 exercise order (Joseph Pilates' Return to Life)
- BASI, Peak Pilates, STOTT Pilates instructor guidance
- Biomechanical warmup requirements
- Spinal mobility and joint readiness demands

**Scoring Criteria:**
- intensity_score: 1-10 scale (1 = cold start OK, 10 = requires full warm-up)
- class_phase: warm_up (1-3), early_middle (4-6), peak (7-9), cool_down (2-4)

## Summary Stats:
- **Warm-up phase (1-3):** 7 movements
- **Early-middle phase (4-6):** 14 movements
- **Peak phase (7-9):** 13 movements
- **Cool-down capable (2-4):** 5 movements (overlap with warm-up)

## Key Research Findings:
1. **The Hundred** - Traditionally first but "can be hard on cold neck/back" per research
2. **Swan Dive** - Research explicitly states "requires warm-up" and spinal prep
3. **Jack Knife** - Position 20/34, requires Roll Over mastery first
4. **Teaser** - Not a "peak pose" in yoga sense but high intensity/strength demand
5. **Control Balance** - Position 32/34, most advanced, requires full prep

## Notes:
- Classical order correlates with intensity but not perfectly (e.g., Roll Over is #34 but high intensity)
- Instructor feedback identified Teaser, Swan Dive, Jack Knife as problematic when placed early
- Some movements (Spine Stretch, Saw, Seal) are safe for both warm-up and cool-down
